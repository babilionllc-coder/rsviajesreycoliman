Requirements to Travel Abroad from Mexico 2026 — Complete Guide

📅 April 25, 2026 ✏️ RS Viajes Rey Colimán Team ⏱️ 11 min read 🔄 Updated 2026
Quick answer: Mexican citizens traveling abroad in 2026 need: a valid passport (6+ months remaining validity), ETIAS authorization for Europe ($7 EUR), country-specific visa where required (USA, China, Russia, etc.), travel insurance (€30,000+ for the Schengen Area), proof of return ticket, and hotel bookings. Travel rules change every year. ETIAS launched in 2026, US visa wait times exploded, and several Asian and Middle-Eastern countries now require online travel authorizations. This RS Viajes Rey Colimán guide is the up-to-date, country-by-country checklist for Mexicans planning international trips in 2026.

📜 Document #1 — Your Mexican Passport

Your passport is the foundation of every international trip. Verify these basics before booking anything else:

  • Validity: at least 6 months remaining from your return date.
  • Free pages: at least 2 blank pages for visa and entry stamps.
  • Damage: no torn pages, water damage, or unreadable photo. Border agents reject damaged passports.
  • Type: ordinary tourist passport is enough for almost all leisure travel.

Renewal takes 2-4 weeks at any SRE delegation in Mexico. Express renewal (24-72 hours) is available for an extra fee. Apply online at citas.sre.gob.mx.

🇪🇺 ETIAS — New Requirement for Europe in 2026

Starting in 2026, Mexicans entering the 29 Schengen Area countries need ETIAS travel authorization. It is electronic, costs €7 EUR (waived for under-18 and over-70), and is valid for 3 years or until your passport expires (whichever comes first).

Apply online at travel-europe.europa.eu/etias_en. Approval is usually instant; some cases take 96 hours. Apply at least 1 week before your trip.

See our full guide: Schengen + ETIAS for Mexicans 2026.

💡 ETIAS is NOT a visa — it is a travel authorization. Mexicans still do not need a tourist visa for stays up to 90 days in the Schengen Area.

🇺🇸 US Tourist Visa (B1/B2) — The Hardest Document in 2026

Mexicans need a B1/B2 tourist visa to enter the USA. Apply at the US Embassy or Consulate.

⚠️ Real wait times (April 2026): First-time appointments at US Embassies in Mexico are 12-18 months. Plan accordingly. Renewals (if previous visa is less than 4 years expired) can sometimes be done by mail without an in-person interview.

Required: appointment at ais.usvisa-info.com, $185 USD payment, DS-160 form, valid passport, photo, financial proof, and travel itinerary.

🛡️ Travel Insurance — Mandatory for Some Countries

Travel insurance is legally required for some destinations and strongly recommended for all international travel:

  • Schengen Area: mandatory €30,000 medical coverage minimum.
  • Cuba: mandatory $10,000 USD coverage minimum.
  • Russia, UAE, Qatar, China: required at visa stage.
  • Everywhere else: not mandatory but strongly recommended (a US hospital night without insurance averages $5,000 USD).

💉 Vaccinations

No vaccines are mandatory for Europe, USA or Canada in 2026. The following may be required for specific destinations:

  • Yellow Fever: required for parts of South America (Peru, Bolivia, Brazilian Amazon) and Africa. Apply at SSA at least 10 days before travel.
  • COVID-19: no longer mandatory anywhere, but cruises sometimes still ask for proof.
  • Recommended for Asia: Hepatitis A and Typhoid (especially India, Indonesia).

✅ Final Pre-Travel Checklist

  • Passport with 6+ months remaining validity and 2 blank pages
  • ETIAS / visa for your destination, approved before flight
  • Travel insurance with documented medical coverage
  • Round-trip flight tickets (printed and on phone)
  • Hotel bookings (printed and on phone)
  • Cash + 2 credit cards in different bags
  • Photocopy of passport stored separately
  • Vaccinations applicable to your destination
  • Emergency contacts (family + Mexican consulate)
  • Phone international roaming or local SIM plan

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What documents do Mexicans need to travel abroad in 2026?

Mexicans need a valid passport (6+ months validity), ETIAS for Europe ($7 EUR), country-specific visa where required (USA, China, Russia), travel insurance (€30,000+ for Schengen), return-ticket proof, and hotel bookings.

Do Mexicans need a US visa?

Yes. Mexicans need a B1/B2 visa for the USA. As of April 2026, first-time appointments are 12-18 months out at US Embassies in Mexico. Renewals can sometimes be processed by mail.

Do Mexicans need a visa for Europe?

No, Mexicans do not need a Schengen visa for tourist stays up to 90 days. From 2026, ETIAS authorization is required (electronic, $7 EUR, valid 3 years).

How long does it take to renew a Mexican passport?

Standard renewal takes 2-4 weeks. Express renewal (24-72 hours) is available for an extra fee. Apply at any SRE delegation or online at citas.sre.gob.mx.
