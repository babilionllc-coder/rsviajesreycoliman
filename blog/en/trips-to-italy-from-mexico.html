🏛️ Must-See Cities — Where to Go in Italy

Rome — 3 nights

The capital deserves at least 3 full days. Must-see: Vatican Museums + Sistine Chapel, Colosseum + Roman Forum, Trevi Fountain, Pantheon, Trastevere neighborhood. Book Vatican tickets online 2 weeks in advance to skip 3-hour lines.

Florence — 2-3 nights

Renaissance capital and gateway to Tuscany. Highlights: Uffizi Gallery (book online), Duomo, Ponte Vecchio, Boboli Gardens. Day trips to Pisa, Siena, Chianti vineyards.

Venice — 2 nights

St Mark's Square, Doge's Palace, gondola ride at sunset, Murano (glass) and Burano (colored houses) islands. Avoid August — heavily crowded and humid.

Amalfi Coast or Cinque Terre — 2-3 nights (optional)

Add for honeymoon or 15-day trips. Cinque Terre = 5 villages connected by hiking trails on the Ligurian coast. Amalfi = Positano, Sorrento, Capri — more luxurious and accessible.

Naples + Pompeii — 1-2 nights (optional)

Birthplace of pizza and gateway to Pompeii ruins and Mount Vesuvius. The most authentic and chaotic city in Italy.

📅 When to Travel to Italy from Mexico

Best months: April-May and September-October — pleasant weather (18-25°C), fewer crowds, and prices 25% below summer peak.

Avoid: August (heavy crowds + many locals on vacation, smaller restaurants closed). High humidity in Venice and Rome.

Cheapest: January-March (excluding Carnival in Venice) — prices 30-40% below August. Cool weather but most museums and restaurants are open with no lines.

Christmas / New Year: magical but premium prices and hotels book 4+ months in advance.

🛂 Documents — What Mexicans Need for Italy 2026

  • Passport with at least 6 months remaining validity from return date.
  • ETIAS travel authorization (€7 EUR, valid 3 years, mandatory from 2026 — see our Schengen + ETIAS guide for Mexicans).
  • Travel insurance with €30,000+ medical coverage (Schengen requirement — see our travel insurance guide).
  • Hotel and flight bookings (sometimes requested at customs).
  • Return ticket proof of departure.
💡 No tourist visa is required for stays up to 90 days. ETIAS is electronic — apply online at travel-europe.europa.eu/etias_en, takes 10 minutes, approval is usually instant.

✈️ Flights from Mexico to Italy

Round-trip Mexico City to Rome / Milan: $750-$1,300 USD in 2026. Best airlines: Aeroméxico (direct seasonally to Rome), Iberia (via Madrid), Air France (via Paris), Lufthansa (via Frankfurt), KLM (via Amsterdam). Flights from Guadalajara typically connect through MEX or Madrid.

Total flight time: 12-15 hours including layover. Book 4-6 months ahead for best fares.

🍝 What to Eat — Italy Food Tips

Italy varies dramatically by region: pasta carbonara in Rome, pizza Margherita in Naples, risotto in Milan, gelato everywhere. Average meal cost: $18-$30 USD for lunch, $30-$50 USD for dinner at a regular trattoria. Avoid restaurants with photos in the menu — tourist trap.

Coffee: at the bar (counter) it costs €1.20; sitting down it can triple. Standing at the counter is the local way.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a trip to Italy from Mexico cost in 2026?

A 12-day trip to Italy from Mexico costs between $1,950 and $4,500 USD per person ($35,000-$85,000 MXN), depending on season, hotel category and inclusions.

When is the best time to visit Italy from Mexico?

April-May and September-October are the best months — pleasant weather, fewer crowds, and prices below peak summer. Avoid August (heavy crowds, many local closures). January-March is the cheapest (-30%).

Do Mexicans need a visa for Italy?

No. Mexicans do not need a Schengen visa for tourist stays up to 90 days. From 2026 you must hold ETIAS (€7 EUR, valid 3 years). Passport must have at least 6 months remaining validity.
